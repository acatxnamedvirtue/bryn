EXPERIENCES WITH TRANS/HIV HEALTH CARE IN NYC: PART 1

The other day I nearly ran out of my HIV meds and I called my pharmacist to ask about a refill. She called me back and told me that my doctor would deign to refill my prescription, but would write no more refills unless she saw me within a month. She insists I go for “check ups” – these last about 20 minutes, during which she is generally like, TAKE YOUR MEDS, and OH, YOU HAVE ANXIETY? YOU SHOULD TRY MEDITATION – about four times a year.

So I called The Big Trans Health Clinic, Inc. (everybody in NY knows exactly where I’m talking about) on November 2nd and asked to make an appointment with my doctor. The receptionist said, “Hmm… the earliest we have available is on New Year’s Eve? Could you come in then?”

Clearly this presents a problem, because I need to take these meds EVERY DAY or I will develop resistance to them and have to change regimens, which could lead to (even more) highly unpleasant side-effects.

I believe that Big Trans Health Clinic, Inc, while being a great resource for many people (including a certain kind of queer – I will go into that more later) has overextended itself. It is over capacity; it has taken on too many clients and cannot care for the volume of patients that depend on them for ALL their health needs.

BUT THIS STORY DOES NOT HAVE AN UNHAPPY ENDING. I found a great alternative health care center which I am very excited about, and will tell you all about in a future post.
