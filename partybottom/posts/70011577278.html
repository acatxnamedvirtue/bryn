Tell us more about yourself? What is your age, where you grew up, favorite color, sweet thing?

partybottom now struggles with two conflicting impulses: loving to talk about herself, and not wanting to blow up her spot too much, because HIV stigma is still very, very real. but here are a few things:

  • i am in my early thirties. (tw: boring queer/trans cred story ahead): i came out as gay when i was 13, started going to gay bars/hanging out with drag queens/dressing up in drag when i was 16, and started on hormones around the age of 20. in fact, i just passed my 11 year anniversary of HRT. whoo hoo?

  • i grew up in the US, mostly in the south and some in the midwest — all over appalachia, basically. my dad was a construction worker and he moved around a lot. my mom was a public school teacher so she did not. they were divorced. we were working class, but not poor. like, think the roseanne show before she got transphobic. i am white. 

  • I started hanging out in/coming to New York about twelve years ago, and moved here full time six years ago. i primarily consider myself an artist/writer. i am on welfare.(welfare is something i really need to talk about on this blog).and have a couple of side hustles to make a little extra money. i have done sex work in the past (cam and escorting), but i do no longer. i am not anti-sex work; it’s just not for me, and i try to do anything i can to avoid having to do it. 

  • i have volunteered at the sylvia rivera law project for the past six years, and am currently part of their trans medicaid access campaign. i also try to support events that raise money for other racial/social justice/hiv/harm reduction orgs like: the audre lorde project, FIERCE, (the now defunct) queers for economic justice, VOCAL: the new york user’s union, and the nyc harm reduction coalition, etc. /boring activist resume.

  • my favorite color is purple! specifically, dusty lady mary crawley purples. my friend today told me that i have “good branding and a strong visual language that i may not recognize” because i have basically been using this same color scheme since my livejournal in 2003;
