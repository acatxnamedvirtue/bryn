STUDY: Gay Men Aren’t Sleeping Around As Much As They Used To

A new study suggests that gay men generally have “significantly fewer” sexual partners than they did 10 years ago. Comparing data from National Surveys of Family Growth in 2002 and between 2006-10, the study shows a consistent decline in sexual partners across most ethnicities and age groups, particularly men under the age of 24.
