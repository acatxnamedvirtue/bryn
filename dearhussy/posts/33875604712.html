I had this dream last night that because of some weird rent-paying scheme machinations, my roommates announced that they had invited my ex to live with us. They announced it at a big house meeting and he was there.

I confronted him and screamed at him. “What is wrong with you? What the fuck are you thinking??”

He said, “I knew you would throw a fit. I actually made a pro and con list, to help me decide whether or not to take this step. Look at my biggest ‘con’: She will be cruël to me. And here, 'she’ means you.”

And he actually showed me the list, and on the list, he had written it just like that. “Cruël.” With an umlaut, like they do in The New Yorker. Except they do not write it like “cruël” in The New Yorker, because the umlaut denotes an extra syllable where you might think there wouldn’t be one, and only total tools pronounce “cruel” with two syllables. 

The moral of the story is, don’t ever ever ever date poets.
