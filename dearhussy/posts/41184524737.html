Not to tell tales out of school, but a certain acerbic cultural critic and colleague of mine at Prettyqueer.com occasionally makes reference to a phenomenon both seemingly spontaneous and curiously collective he calls

“femmes who look like Fred Flintstone.”

I can now never unsee it. Perhaps this has called up, in your mind’s eye, the image of one (or more) of your own homophile acquaintances. Now you will never be able to unsee it.

As Helen Gurley Brown says on the mutable nature of bisexuality in her seminal work, Having It All, “I am not saying there is anything wrong with all of this. I am merely pointing it out.”
